**Types of Healthcare Reports**

Some of the medical reports that I found
- Foundational Reports : These include reports contain the objective information about a patients symptoms. They have the following types - 
  - Laboratory Reports: These include an examination of your bodily fluids (blood cell count, other chemical levels, etc.)
  - Radiology Reports: These are detailed description of imaging repots like MRI, CT Scan and X-Ray given by Radiologists
  - Pathology Reports: Reports created by pathologists after doing a detailed biopsy on a tissue of a patient.


**Medical Imaging Reports**

Medical imaging is a non-invasive technology that acquires signals by leveraging the physical principles of sound, light, electromagnetic wave, etc., from which visual images of internal tissues of the human body are generated. There are many widely used medical imaging modalities, including ultrasound, digital radiography, computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and optical coherent tomography (OCT).According to a report, about 90% of all healthcare data are medical images, which undoubtedly become a critical source of evidence for clinical decision making, such as diagnosis and intervention. Traditionally these medical images were analysed by radiologists who used to write reports but due to the onset of technology they have been aided through the help of deep learning models like CNNs or more recently Vision Transformers
